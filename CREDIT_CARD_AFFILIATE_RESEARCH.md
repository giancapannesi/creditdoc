## Executive Summary

Credit card affiliate marketing is the highest-paying vertical in all of affiliate marketing. Individual card approvals pay $25-$405 per conversion. However, most of those high payouts are for premium travel and business cards (Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum) that require 700+ credit scores -- exactly the audience CreditDoc does NOT serve.

**The honest reality for CreditDoc's rebuilder audience (300-700 FICO):**

- Secured card commissions: $25-$45 per funded account
- Subprime unsecured card commissions: $30-$40 per approval
- Credit repair commissions: $65-$120 per paid subscriber (some recurring)
- Credit monitoring commissions: $3-$30 per signup
- Credit builder loan commissions: $10 per account

The lower per-conversion payouts for secured/subprime cards are offset by MUCH higher approval rates (some secured cards approve 95%+ since they require a deposit, not a credit check). This means more of your traffic converts.

**Key insight:** CreditDoc should NOT chase the high-CPA premium card programs. The audience mismatch would kill conversion rates. Instead, focus on high-volume secured cards + credit repair + credit monitoring as a stack. A visitor who signs up for credit monitoring ($3-$6) today may also apply for a secured card ($25-$45) and enroll in credit repair ($65-$120). That is $93-$171 per engaged visitor across multiple products.

## CPL vs CPA: Which Model for CreditDoc

This is a critical strategic decision for CreditDoc's rebuilder audience.

### CPA (Cost Per Approval) Model
- Pays $25-$200+ per APPROVED application
- Higher payout per conversion
- Risk: if your audience has bad credit, approval rates are low
- Example: 100 clicks -> 15 apply -> 3 approved at $100 = $300

### CPL (Cost Per Lead) Model
- Pays $5-$35 per SUBMITTED application (regardless of approval)
- Lower payout per conversion
- Benefit: every application counts, no approval dependency
- Example: 100 clicks -> 15 apply at $25 = $375

### Recommendation for CreditDoc

**For secured cards:** Use CPA. Secured cards have 90-95% approval rates because they require a deposit, not a credit check. You get paid on almost every application.

**For unsecured subprime cards:** Use CPA where available, but consider CPL alternatives. Approval rates for subprime unsecured cards (Indigo, Surge, Milestone) are lower but still reasonable (50-70% for the target demographic).

**For credit repair:** CPA. These are service signups, not credit applications. The "approval" is just paying for the first month.

**For credit monitoring:** CPL or per-signup. These are free or trial-based, so conversion friction is low.

## Compliance Requirements

Credit card affiliate marketing is a regulated activity. CreditDoc MUST comply with these rules.

### FTC Requirements

1. **Clear affiliate disclosures** on every page containing affiliate links
2. Disclosures must be "clear and conspicuous" -- not buried in footer text
3. Must be near the affiliate link, not on a separate disclosures page
4. Must use plain language: "We may earn a commission if you apply through links on this page"
5. Cannot make misleading claims about approval odds
6. Cannot guarantee specific credit outcomes

### FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act) Affiliate Marketing Rules

1. Cannot use consumer credit information received from an affiliate to make marketing solicitations without opt-out notice
2. Must comply with affiliate marketing opt-out requirements

### Credit CARD Act of 2009

1. Truthful representation of card terms (APR, fees, rewards)
2. Cannot obscure or minimize material terms

### Financial Institution Liability

Banks hold their affiliates accountable. If CreditDoc makes misleading claims, the bank can:
- Terminate the affiliate relationship immediately
- Claw back commissions
- Pursue legal action

### CreditDoc Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Add affiliate disclosure to every page with affiliate links
- [ ] Add disclosure to site-wide header or top of content area
- [ ] Never state or imply guaranteed approval
- [ ] Always show accurate card terms (APR, annual fee, deposit requirements)
- [ ] Never misrepresent credit score requirements
- [ ] Maintain separate editorial policy page
- [ ] Review content quarterly for compliance
